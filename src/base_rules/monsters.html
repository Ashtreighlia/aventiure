the monsters of âventiure

Since this version is still an early alpha, there is no monster compendium - yet.

As a guide to creating monsters in âventiure, the following chapters describe a rough framework within which you can design your encounters.

stats

level

The level of a monster determines all of its major stats, like health, number of actions per turn and the attack/defense levels. Generally meaning the higher the level of a monster, the more difficult it gets to defeat it.

tier

A monster can fall into different tiers. You can think of it from minion (tier 1) to boss (tier 5), but they are all from the same species. However, the tier of a monster only affects its health and not any other stats.

health

The health of a monster is being calculated by its base health, level and tier.

base_health = 50 level = X * d10-roll tier = Y * 25 health = base_health + level + tier

combat

actions

The number of acitons per turn for a monster are determined by its level. For example, a level 5 monster would have 5 actions per turn.

movement

Every monster has two movement actions, but they are also allowed to use their normal actions as a movement action.

attack and defence

A monster's attack and defence level is the same as the level of the monster. But the same action-decay rules apply here, meaning, that the attack level decrases with every actionin the same turn.

damage

The damage a monster can deal is the sum of rolled Xd10, where X is the level of the monster.
