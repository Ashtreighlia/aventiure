the rules of âventiure

This is the basic set of rules for âventiure. Stick to them, modify them, or completely bend them to your will. Just have fun with the game, your creativity is the limit.

skills

Unlike most rulesets, âventiure does not use predefined skills. The main focus is on developing your character over time. Just like in real life you can do anything - you might suck at it, but at least you can give it a try.

The first skill owned by everyone, is do a thing: 1 - meaning you can do anything as a level 1 action.

When someone wants to do something, they and the game master (GM) roll their d10s and d10.0s dice (for a total of 1-100) to decide whether the player was successful. For example, if you want to open a LVL 1 lock with a LVL 1 lock picking skill, both roll once. However, if higher levels are involved, both roll as many times as they have levels in their "skill". The highest single roll of both parties counts. If the player wins, he manages to do whatever he wanted to do. However, if the GM wins, the player screws up his action. If there is a tie on the highest roll, the player succeeds.

If a player rolls a repdigit (a 100 counts as a repdigit -> 00-0), he is granted a level up in whatever he was just trying to do. However he needs to throw as many repdigits as he made rolls, meaning, that for a level 3 skill the player has to roll 3 repdigits in a row while performing his action to level up his skill.

tools

A player can find and use tools to get an edge over the GM. They increase the likely hood of them succeeding. For more information look up the armoury of âventiure.

combat

initiative

The order in combat is determined by a d10 roll. If the GM and a player happen to roll the same value, the player goes first. However, if multiple players roll the same value, they can decide who goes first.

actions

Every player has 2 actions and 2 movement action in combat. They can use their actions however they want, but they cannot do the same action and skill level twice per turn. Attacking, casting a spell, using a skill or even using their normal actions as movement actions counts towards their overall used actions that turn. You can only use a particular skill level once per turn, which means that any subsequent actions must be made with the next lower skill level. This also applies to other skills, attack skills or spell skills in your turn, meaning that you should start with the highest skill level and work your way down from there.

movement

Every player can move 1 block per used movement action. As a measurement for distance you may choose any measurement system that fits your bill (should be around 1 meters, 3 feet or 60 barleycorn per block).

attack

Everyone can attack a thing: 1. Just like with the normal skills, you can attack anything you want, however you want, but don't expect to impress a dragon by hitting it with a rock. Here, as with a skill check, the attacker rolls their d10s and d10.0s (for a total of 1-100) according to their skill level for their attack and the defender rolls with their defence skill against the attackers roll. If the attacker wins, the target takes the damage described by their weapon. Otherwise the attacker has missed their target. As with the skills before, you increase your skill level by rolling repdigits.

defend

When defending, each player initially resorts to defend a thing: 1. This is the player's defence against an attack as described earlier in the paragraph attack. The ability improves by rolling repdigits for the type of armour used.

weapons and armour

By using better weapons and armour the player can get an advantage in combat. The damage of a weapon aswell as the defence of armour is determined by its quality.

For more information look up the armoury of âventiure.

magic

As in the previous chapters, spells also have skill levels. Each player knows a number of spells based on their class and race. They can cast all of these as level 1 spells. Every player has a certain amount of mana points according to their picked class and race. Casting a spell uses up one mana point, if you are out of mana points you cannot cast any more spells. Mana points can be refreshed by taking a rest or a mana potion.

spells

There are three different types of spells - attack, support and utility. Utility and support spells are cast against the worst d10 and d10.0 (for 1-100) roll of the GM with the same amount of dice as the player has skill levels. Attack spells, on the other hand, are always rolled against the defence level of the attacked, the highest single roll counts. In the event of a tie, the attacked wins. The damage is determined by the wand used and the properties of the spell.

rituals

Unlike spells, rituals are not directl magical influences emanating from a being, but are rather created through concentration. Therefore, rituals do not require checks, but they can only be prepared during a rest. Depending on the choosen class and race a player can prepare multiple rituals per rest.

For more information look up the magic of âventiure.

characters

Like any other TTRPG, âventiure also relies heavily on its characters.

classes and races

When creating a character, both their class and race are important for their traits. âventiure tries to keep the system relatively simple so as not to waste too much time (subsequent characters should therefore be relatively easy to create.. dying is part of the game ^^).

For more information look up the characters of âventiure.

character level up

Levelling is mostly determined by the GM. As a rule of thumb you level up every 6 skill levels you learned as a character. A character level up grants you full health, mana points and can be regarded as a rest with a consumed ration. Additionally you gain a permanent 1d10 to your maximum health and one additional mana point. Every 2 character level ups you learn one new spell or ritual.

healing

Healing can be achieved in four different ways. Either by casting a heal spell, using a potion of healing (heals 1/4 of maximum HP) or treating your wounds. Treating woulds does 1d10 healing, if the skill check was successful. However, doing this will use all you actions of your turn in combat. Alernatively, you can take a rest with a consumed ration to fully heal yourself.

resting

You can take a rest at any time, but you should seek a safe place first to set up camp. A rest takes around 6 hours and refills all your mana points. If you have consumed a ration your health will be completely restored, this is optional, but highly recommended.

death

Dying is always such a thing. No one wants to, but someone has to. In âventiure, death is handled relatively simply. If you are at 0HP, you get unconscious. In âventiure you only die if an enemy attacks and hits you while you are unconscious. In order to revive a teammate you have to healing them, be it by treating their wounds, casting a healing spell or giving them a healing potion.

monsters

Since âventiure is a very open game system, the monsters you use in your session are as freely inventable as the rest of the game. So go nuts! In the following, we will roughly go into the values such as health and actions per battle as well as attacks, damage and defence of monsters.

For more information look up the monsters of âventiure.
